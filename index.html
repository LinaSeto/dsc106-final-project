Alarming Coral-ations: How Climate Change Impacts Life Under The Sea

The reef remembers
every degree.

Between 2002 and 2024, the Great Barrier Reef endured six mass bleaching events — more than in the previous century combined. The water it lives in warmed by less than two degrees. This is the story of that small number, and what it took with it.

Where the reefs are

Coral reefs cover less than one percent of the ocean floor, yet they shelter roughly a quarter of all marine species. They cluster in a narrow tropical band — warm enough to grow, cool enough to survive.

One reef, the size of a country

The Great Barrier Reef stretches 2,300 kilometres along Australia's northeast coast. From orbit, it is the largest living structure on Earth.

A baseline temperature

For most of the satellite era, sea surface temperature here held remarkably steady. Corals built their skeletons against a familiar rhythm of seasons.
